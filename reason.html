Why stay up late?

  There are always some reasons that keep us up late and most of them are likely to be experienced by us in this modern life.

Distraction
There are too many distractions challenging us in modern life. It is tempting to watch dramas and scroll through your social media feed before sleeping. These may distract you for hours and keep yourself from a restful night.Your mind can stay active and engaged long after you've scrolled through social media apps or responded to your work emails.

Too many thoughts
Sometimes, you will feel like you can't get your brain to shut up for you to fall asleep. Your're mentally reviewing the incidents happened as well as previewing what would happen next! Sleeping is the time for us to escape from all the troubles we meet in a day, so we should stop worrying about all the works and keep our computer away from the bed.

Bad Sleeping Habit
Bad sleeping habit may affect your sleeping. One must get adequate nighttime sleep and set a consistent wake-up time to enhance a healthy sleeping routin. Your naps can be between 50 minutes to 4 hours long. As your afternoon napping time goes longer, your daytime sleepiness will get worse because it can interfere with your nighttime sleep.

Hectic Schedule
Students that have a packed course load may have to stay up late in the night. Not only they have to finish their assignments or homework but also studying for their test or final exams. Also, some of them have to work a part-time job attend social events or even sports. 24 hours might seem enough for them in order to complete all the tasks if one procrastinates. Eventually, they have to burn the midnight oil.

Sleep Disorder
Sleep disorder are conditions that cause changes in the way you sleep. There are many types of sleep disorders. Some common types of sleep disorders include Insomnia, Sleep Apnea, Restless Legs Syndrome (RLS) and Narcolepsy. Symptoms of sleep disorders include being very sleepy during daytime and having difficulty in falling asleep at night, breathing in an unusual pattern and feeling an uncomfortable urge to move while you're trying to fall asleep.

  There are a variety of reasons to stay up late but you should do that in a healthier way.
